In the bustling streets of Ruwi, the upscale avenues of Al Khuwair, and the vibrant markets of Seeb, your signboard is more than just a marker—it is your first handshake with the customer. However, in Muscat, that signboard represents a legal contract between your business and the city.

Muscat favors a curated, harmonious aesthetic called Al‑Mandhar Al‑Aam (The General Appearance). For business owners, this means that “just putting up a sign” is not an option. The Muscat Municipality, with MoCIIP, enforces rigorous standards to keep the city pristine.

In 2025, the rules have evolved. Digital government services streamlined the process, but technical requirements remain strict. A single mistake—Arabic translation errors, structural encroachment, or missing the correct license—can lead to fines, removals, and delays. This guide explains the rules, the new OMR 15 fee, and how to navigate Invest Easy for first‑try approval.

1. The Regulatory Shift: From Municipality to “Invest Easy”

The One‑Stop‑Shop revolution. Previously, permits required physical visits to regional directorates. Today, the process is integrated into the Invest Easy platform and tied to your Commercial Registration (CR) and licensed activity.

Workflow. When you register a new activity or branch, “Commercial Signboard Request” appears as a sub‑service. The sign text must strictly match your licensed activity. Inspectors will flag mismatches.

Baladiyeti’s role. Invest Easy handles initiation and payment; Baladiyeti remains the technical engine for complex amendments, temporary banners, or engineer interactions.

2. Technical Specifications: The “Pass/Fail” Criteria

The 18‑Meter Rooftop Rule. Installing advertising signs on rooftops of buildings less than 18 meters high is prohibited. Low‑rise areas prioritize safety and visual harmony. Standard G+2 buildings are typically under 18 meters—stick to fascia signs.

Arabic as primary. Literary Arabic is mandatory; English is secondary. The Arabic text should be primary in placement/size and must match the Arabic trade name on your CR. Poor automatic translations are rejected.

Safety & obstruction (Article 8 & 124). Signs must not block windows, balconies, or ventilation; must leave pedestrian clearance; and cannot include flashing/strobing lights that distract drivers.

3. Financial Implications: Fees vs. Fines

New fee structure. A flat OMR 15 issuance fee for standard commercial signboards—often one‑time, provided the sign does not change.

Penalty regime. Unlicensed signs: OMR 50–100, doubled if repeated. Waste disposal violations after install: OMR 100 (repeat: OMR 300). Distorting the general appearance (faded/defective signs) can lead to fines and municipal removal at your cost.

4. The Application Process: A Step‑by‑Step Guide

  1. Pre‑requisites: Valid CR, registered tenancy, and a landlord NOC permitting a signboard.
  2. Visualization: A realistic render on the actual building photo with precise dimensions and placement. Poor visuals are rejected.
  3. Portal submission: In Invest Easy, choose “License Services” → “Commercial Signboard Request” and upload documents.
  4. Review & payment: Engineers verify Arabic text, dimensions, and safety; upon approval, pay the OMR 15 fee.
  5. Fabrication & installation: Only after approval. Installing first risks removals or re‑fabrication.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I use a digital screen (LED video wall) for my shop sign?

Generally, no. Moving video or flashing screens are restricted for street‑facing shop fronts to prevent driver distraction. They are usually only permitted in specific pedestrian zones or malls, and require special high‑level approvals.

My shop is in a side street. Do I really need a license?

Yes. Article 5 applies to all commercial advertisements, regardless of visibility. Municipal inspectors patrol all zones. An unlicensed sign in an alley is still a violation.

How long does the approval process take?

With a correct submission via Invest Easy, approval can be as fast as 3–5 working days. Errors in the visualization or Arabic text can extend this to weeks.

Can I put my sign in a different language like Hindi or Urdu?

Arabic (primary) and English (secondary) are the norms. Other languages are generally not permitted on the main commercial signboard, with rare, strictly regulated exceptions.
